Recipients

Nambucca Valley Council, NSW Department of Climate Change, Energy, the Environment and Water, and Commonwealth Department of Climate Change, Energy and Water are the primary recipients. The notice is framed as non-allegatory and describes system behaviour rather than individual intent.

Identification of harm

Taylors Arm is described as a village of 133 people without reticulated town water. The primary local economic asset is the Pub With No Beer. The recorded harms include tank drinking water quality contamination, soiled washing, foul odour, vehicle paintwork damage, and loss of usable outdoor space.

Documented non-response

The council record reviewed spans March 2023 to April 2026. The notice states that no resolution, referral, action item, staff direction, response, staff assessment, management plan, camp registration action, or grant application appears in the public record for the Taylors Arm issue.

Legislative basis

The notice identifies intersecting obligations and frameworks: Biosecurity Act 2015 for Camphor Laurel weed control, NSW Flying-Fox Camp Management Policy 2015, Flying-Fox Camp Management Code of Practice 2018, Local Government Act strategic directions, and Commonwealth EPBC responsibilities for the grey-headed flying fox and national monitoring architecture.

Remedy demands

Nambucca Valley Council

NVC-1 acknowledge the March 2023 presentation and confirm action taken or proposed. NVC-2 notify the camp to the National Flying-Fox Monitoring Program. NVC-3 commence a Review of Environmental Factors for the Camphor Laurel trees with a qualified ecologist. NVC-4 apply for grant funding after camp registration. NVC-5 develop a camp management plan in consultation with the affected community.

NSW DCCEEW

NSW-1 provide direct advice and support to council. NSW-2 confirm whether the Taylors Arm camp is registered and initiate registration if not. NSW-3 confirm the current grant-program status and advise council on access steps.

Commonwealth DCCEEW

CWLTH-1 confirm whether the camp is known to the National Flying-Fox Monitoring Program and ensure assessment for inclusion if not. CWLTH-2 confirm the current monitoring program review status and how unregistered camps can be included in the successor monitoring architecture.

Regional corridor finding

The notice identifies Taylors Arm and Bellbrook as adjacent hinterland communities with equivalent grey-headed flying fox governance failure patterns: no management plan, no documented grant access, and council records that either fail to document an active camp or fail to document any response to a community complaint.
