Core evidence chain

EVID-001 to EVID-007 — March 2023 council meeting

Vicki Provost registered to address Nambucca Valley Council on “Flying Fox Infestation Taylors Arm Village”. The meeting was held at Taylors Arm Hall. The minutes recorded colony growth, Camphor Laurel roosting, tank drinking water quality issues, soiled washing, odour, vehicle paint damage, and the request about removal of Camphor Laurel trees. No resolution, referral, action item, or staff direction was recorded after the presentation.

EVID-008, EVID-009, EVID-026, EVID-027 — response void

Outstanding Actions registers and later council records contain no entry for flying foxes, Taylors Arm infestation, Camphor Laurel removal, or action arising from the March 2023 public forum presentation.

EVID-010 to EVID-012; EVID-033 to EVID-037 — Camphor Laurel and biosecurity

Nambucca Valley Council policy, NSW WeedWise, and the North Coast Regional Strategic Weed Management Plan identify Camphor Laurel as a weed control target/species of concern and identify Local Control Authority weed functions under the Biosecurity Act 2015.

EVID-013 to EVID-016 — precedents and corridor pattern

Nambucca previously developed a Gordon Park flying fox management plan in 2017. Kempsey Shire conducted a Review of Environmental Factors and engaged an ecologist for Camphor Laurel removals in 2024. Bellbrook is documented as a parallel hinterland flying fox case with budget-denial and management-plan failure.

EVID-021; EVID-038 to EVID-059 — National Flying-Fox Monitoring Program

The former web viewer was decommissioned on 25 June 2025. Camp data moved to the CSIRO Data Access Portal. Public notification channels include speciespolicy@environment.gov.au and CSIRO contacts David Westcott and Adam McKeown.

EVID-065 to EVID-072 — protected species and camp management framework

The grey-headed flying fox is listed as Vulnerable under Commonwealth and NSW frameworks. Actions likely to significantly affect the species may require Commonwealth referral. NSW policy strongly encourages camp management plans and identifies consultation and code-of-practice pathways.
